### **Original LTL Formula:**
**(c U a) & (b U a)**

---

### **Parsing Tree Breakdown:**
The formula is a conjunction (`&`) of two **Until (U)** subformulas:
1. **(c U a)**
   - Left operand: **c** (atomic proposition)
   - Right operand: **a** (atomic proposition)
   - Operator: **U** (strong Until)

2. **(b U a)**
   - Left operand: **b** (atomic proposition)
   - Right operand: **a** (atomic proposition)
   - Operator: **U** (strong Until)

The top-level operator is **& (AND)**, meaning **both** subformulas must hold simultaneously.

---

### **Rebuilding Meaning from the Bottom Up:**

#### **1. Understanding (c U a):**
The **Until** operator **(c U a)** means:
> *"**c** must hold **continuously** **until** a point where **a** becomes true, and **a** must eventually occur."*

- **Satisfaction conditions:**
  - At every state where **a** is **not yet true**, **c** must hold.
  - **a** must eventually become true in the future (the "until" condition is **not** vacuously satisfied if **a** never occurs).
  - Once **a** becomes true, the obligation for **c** ends.

- **When is this violated?**
  - If **c** becomes false **before** **a** becomes true.
  - If **a** **never** becomes true (infinite waiting).

#### **2. Understanding (b U a):**
Similarly, **(b U a)** means:
> *"**b** must hold **continuously** **until** a point where **a** becomes true, and **a** must eventually occur."*

- **Satisfaction conditions:**
  - **b** must remain true in every state until **a** is reached.
  - **a** must eventually happen.

- **When is this violated?**
  - If **b** fails before **a** is true.
  - If **a** never occurs.

#### **3. Combining with & (AND):**
The full formula **(c U a) & (b U a)** requires **both** Until conditions to hold **simultaneously**:
> *"**c** must hold until **a**, **and** **b** must hold until **a**, **and** **a** must eventually occur."*

- **Key implications:**
  - Both **c** and **b** must be true in every state **until** **a** is reached.
  - **a** must eventually become true (terminating the Until obligations).
  - If either **c** or **b** fails before **a** is true, the formula is violated.

---

### **Refined Natural Language Translation:**
Here are progressively clearer phrasings:

1. **Literal Translation:**
   *"It is always the case that **c** holds until **a** becomes true, and **b** holds until **a** becomes true, and **a** must eventually become true."*

2. **More Intuitive Phrasing:**
   *"Both **c** and **b** must remain true **simultaneously** until **a** eventually occurs. Once **a** happens, the requirements for **c** and **b** are lifted."*

3. **System-Level Interpretation (if **a**, **b**, **c** represent events/conditions):**
   *"The system must maintain **condition c** and **condition b** at all times **until** **event a** occurs. **Event a** is guaranteed to happen eventually, at which point **c** and **b** are no longer required."*

4. **Temporal Constraint Summary:**
   - **Invariant until a:** Both **c** and **b** are invariants that must hold in every state where **a** is false.
   - **Liveness:** **a** must eventually become true (no infinite postponement).

---

|-----------------------------|-----------------------------------|-------------|

---
### **Final Answer:**
The LTL formula **(c U a) & (b U a)** means:

> *"Both **c** and **b** must hold **continuously** in every state **until** **a** eventually becomes true. The occurrence of **a** is guaranteed in the future, and once it happens, the requirements for **c** and **b** are no longer enforced."*

### **Key Takeaways:**
1. **Conjunction of Untils:** Both **c** and **b** must persist until **a**.
2. **Termination:** **a** must eventually occur (no infinite waiting).
3. **Violation Cases:**
   - Either **c** or **b** fails before **a**.
   - **a** never happens.
