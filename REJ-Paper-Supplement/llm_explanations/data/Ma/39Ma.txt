Here’s the structured translation of the LTL formula **`b U (d & a)`**:

---

### **1. Original LTL Formula**
**`b U (d & a)`**

---

### **2. Parsing Tree Breakdown**
The formula is a **Until** (`U`) operator with two subformulas:
- **Left operand (antecedent):** `b`
  (An atomic proposition, e.g., "event `b` holds at the current state.")
- **Right operand (consequent):** `(d & a)`
  (A conjunction of two atomic propositions: "both `d` and `a` hold simultaneously.")

Visually:
```
      U
     / \
    b   &
       / \
      d   a
```

---

### **3. Rebuilding Meaning from the Bottom Up**
#### **Step 1: Conjunction `(d & a)`**
- **Meaning:** "At a given state, **both `d` and `a` are true simultaneously**."
- **Example:** If `d` = "door is locked" and `a` = "alarm is active," then `(d & a)` means "the door is locked **and** the alarm is active at the same time."

#### **Step 2: Until Operator `b U (d & a)`**
- **Formal Semantics:** The Until operator `φ U ψ` requires that:
  1. **`ψ` (the consequent) must eventually become true** in some future state.
  2. **Until that happens, `φ` (the antecedent) must hold continuously** in every state along the path.

- **Applied here:**
  - **`(d & a)` must eventually occur** (i.e., there exists a future state where both `d` and `a` are true).
  - **Until that point, `b` must remain true** in every intermediate state.

- **Key Constraints:**
  - If `(d & a)` never becomes true, the formula is **false** (even if `b` holds forever).
  - If `b` becomes false **before** `(d & a)` occurs, the formula is **false** at that point.

#### **Step 3: Temporal Interpretation**
- The formula describes a **bounded waiting condition**:
  - The system must **stay in a state where `b` holds** ("waiting phase").
  - This waiting **ends when `(d & a)` is satisfied** ("termination condition").
  - If the termination condition never arrives, the formula fails.

---

### **4. Refined Natural Language Phrasing**
**Primary Translation:**
> "`b` must hold continuously **until** a state where **both `d` and `a` are true simultaneously** occurs.
> Moreover, `(d & a)` **must eventually happen**—otherwise, the formula is violated."

---
**Alternative Phrasings (Context-Dependent):**
1. **Process-Control View:**
   > "The system must maintain property `b` at all times **until** it reaches a state where `d` and `a` are both active. The latter state **must** be reached eventually."

2. **Event-Driven View:**
   > "Event `b` must persist in every step **until** the combined event `(d and a)` fires. The combined event **is guaranteed to occur** at some point."

3. **Safety-Liveness View:**
   - **Safety:** "`b` is invariant until `(d & a)`."
   - **Liveness:** "`(d & a)` will eventually terminate the waiting period."

---
**When the Formula is Satisfied:**
- ✅ `b` holds in every state from now until some future state where `(d & a)` becomes true.
- ✅ `(d & a)` eventually occurs (even if far in the future).

**When the Formula is Violated:**
- ❌ `b` becomes false **before** `(d & a)` occurs.
- ❌ `(d & a)` **never** occurs (even if `b` holds forever).

---
**Example Scenario:**
- Let:
  - `b` = "brake pedal is pressed,"
  - `d` = "speed < 10 km/h,"
  - `a` = "parking sensor activated."
- **Meaning:**
  > "The brake pedal must stay pressed **until** the car slows below 10 km/h **and** the parking sensor turns on. This combined condition **must** happen eventually."
