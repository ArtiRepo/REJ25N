**Refine the natural language phrasing for clarity:**

The LTL formula `F !b` means:

**"Eventually, `b` will be false."**

**Explanation:**

*   **When the formula is satisfied:** This formula is satisfied if, at some point in the current execution trace (including the current moment or any future moment), the proposition `b` is observed to be false. Once `b` becomes false at a particular state, the formula is considered satisfied for that entire trace, regardless of what happens afterwards.

*   **When it imposes constraints on the system over time:** This formula imposes a constraint that `b` cannot remain true indefinitely. There must be a point in time where `b` transitions from true to false, or it must already be false at the beginning. If `b` is true at the initial state, the system is constrained to eventually reach a state where `b` is false. If `b` is already false at the initial state, the constraint is immediately met. It essentially forbids `b` from being perpetually true.
