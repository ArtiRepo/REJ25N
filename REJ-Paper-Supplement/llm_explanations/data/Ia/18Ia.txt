2.  **`b`**: This proposition is true if condition `b` holds at the *current moment*.
3.  **`(a & b)`**: This subformula is true if *both* condition `a` and condition `b` hold simultaneously at the *current moment*.
    *   *Constraint:* At the current moment, `a` and `b` must both be true.

    *   *Constraint:* There must exist *some* moment in time (now or later) where `a` and `b` are simultaneously true.
    *   *Satisfied when:* We can find at least one state in the current or future path where `a` and `b` are both true.
5.  **`!F (a & b)`**: This means "Not Eventually `(a & b)`". This formula is true if it is *not* the case that `(a & b)` will eventually become true. In other words, `(a & b)` will *never* be true at any point in the future (including the current moment).
    *   *Constraint:* At *no point* in time (now or in the future) should `a` and `b` ever be simultaneously true.
    *   *Satisfied when:* For *every* moment in time, from the current moment onwards, it is *not* the case that both `a` and `b` are true. This means that the state where `a` and `b` are simultaneously true is completely forbidden.

**Refine the Natural Language Phrasing for Clarity:**

The LTL formula `!F (a & b)` means:

**"It is never the case that both `a` and `b` are true simultaneously."**

**Explanation:**

*   **When the formula is satisfied:** The system satisfies this formula if, at no point in its entire execution (starting from the current moment and extending infinitely into the future), do conditions `a` and `b` ever occur at the same time. If you were to observe the system's state at every single moment, you would never find a moment where both `a` and `b` are active.

*   **When it imposes constraints on the system over time:** This formula imposes a **safety property**. It acts as a strict prohibition: the system must be designed and operate in such a way that the state where `a` and `b` are simultaneously true is absolutely forbidden. It ensures that a specific "bad" state (where `a` and `b` both hold) is never reached. For example, if `a` is "door is open" and `b` is "train is moving", then `!F (a & b)` means "the door must never be open while the train is moving."
