### Refine Natural Language Phrasing for Clarity

The LTL formula `!F(a and Xa and XXa)` means:

**"It is never the case that proposition `a` is true for three consecutive time steps."**

In other words:

**"The system must always ensure that `a` is not true for three consecutive moments in time."**

This formula acts as a safety constraint, preventing a specific undesirable sequence of events from ever occurring. If `a` represents a critical condition (e.g., "temperature is too high"), then this formula ensures that the system never allows the temperature to be too high for an extended period of three consecutive time units.
