`(a U b) & (c U d)`

    *   `a` is an atomic proposition.
    *   `b` is an atomic proposition.
    *   `U` is the "Until" operator.
*   Its right operand is the subformula `(c U d)`.
    *   `c` is an atomic proposition.

### Rebuild Meaning from the Bottom Up

Let's define the `U` (Until) operator first, as it's the core of the subformulas.
**`X U Y` (X Until Y):** This formula is true at a given moment if and only if:
1.  `Y` eventually becomes true at some point in the future (including the current moment).
2.  Until `Y` becomes true, `X` must be continuously true in all states strictly before `Y` becomes true. If `Y` is true at the current moment, the "until" condition for `X` is vacuously satisfied for the current moment.
This is often referred to as "strong until" because `Y` is guaranteed to eventually happen.

1.  **Subformula: `(a U b)`**
    *   **Meaning:** From the current moment, `b` must eventually become true. Until `b` becomes true, `a` must be continuously true.
    *   **When satisfied:** This subformula is satisfied if there is a future state (or the current state) where `b` is true, and in all states leading up to that point (from the current state, exclusive of the state where `b` is true), `a` was true.
    *   **Constraints imposed:** It imposes two constraints: an eventuality constraint that `b` *must* happen, and a safety constraint that `a` *must* hold continuously until `b` happens. If `b` never occurs, this subformula is violated.

2.  **Subformula: `(c U d)`**
    *   **Meaning:** From the current moment, `d` must eventually become true. Until `d` becomes true, `c` must be continuously true.
    *   **When satisfied:** This subformula is satisfied if there is a future state (or the current state) where `d` is true, and in all states leading up to that point (from the current state, exclusive of the state where `d` is true), `c` was true.
    *   **Constraints imposed:** Similar to `(a U b)`, it imposes an eventuality constraint that `d` *must* happen, and a safety constraint that `c` *must* hold continuously until `d` happens. If `d` never occurs, this subformula is violated.

3.  **Combining with `&`: `(a U b) & (c U d)`**
    *   **Meaning:** The `&` (AND) operator requires that *both* of its operands must be true simultaneously at the current moment. Therefore, the system must satisfy the conditions for `(a U b)` AND the conditions for `(c U d)`.
    *   **When satisfied:** The entire formula is satisfied if and only if:
        *   `b` eventually becomes true, AND `a` is continuously true until `b` becomes true.
        *   **AND**
        *   `d` eventually becomes true, AND `c` is continuously true until `d` becomes true.
        The eventual occurrences of `b` and `d` do not need to happen at the same time; they can occur at different points in the future, or even simultaneously. The "until" periods for `a` and `c` also operate independently.
    *   **Constraints imposed:** This formula imposes a set of parallel constraints. It demands that the system eventually reaches a state where `b` is true (having maintained `a` until then), AND it also independently demands that the system eventually reaches a state where `d` is true (having maintained `c` until then). If either of these two independent conditions is violated (e.g., `b` never happens, or `c` fails before `d` happens), the entire formula is violated.

1.  **Regarding `a` and `b`:** It must eventually be the case that `b` becomes true. Until that moment, `a` must be continuously true. (If `b` never occurs, this condition is violated.)
2.  **Regarding `c` and `d`:** It must eventually be the case that `d` becomes true. Until that moment, `c` must be continuously true. (If `d` never occurs, this condition is violated.)
