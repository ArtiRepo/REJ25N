### Translation of the LTL Formula: `(c U a) & (b U a)`

#### Step 1: Original LTL Formula  
`(c U a) & (b U a)`  

#### Step 2: Parsing Tree Breakdown  
The formula breaks down as follows:  
```
     &
   /   \
  U     U
 / \   / \
c   a b   a
```  
- **Top-level operator**: `&` (logical AND) combines two subformulas.  
- **Left subformula**: `(c U a)`  
  - Operator: `U` (Until)  
  - Left operands: `c` (atomic proposition)  
  - Right operand: `a` (atomic proposition)  
- **Right subformula**: `(b U a)`  
  - Operator: `U` (Until)  
  - Left operands: `b` (atomic proposition)  
  - Right operand: `a` (atomic proposition)  

#### Step 3: Rebuilding Meaning from the Bottom Up  
1. **Atomic Propositions (`a`, `b`, `c`)**:  
   - `a`: A specific condition must hold at a state.  
   - `b`: Another condition must hold at a state.  
   - `c`: Yet another condition must hold at a state.  

2. **Until Subformulas**:  
   - **`(c U a)`**:  
     - *Interpretation*: "Condition `c` must hold continuously **until** condition `a` becomes true, and `a` **must eventually become true**."  
     - *Example*: If `c` is "system is in setup mode" and `a` is "operation starts," then `(c U a)` requires the system to stay in setup mode until operation starts (with operation eventually starting).  
   - **`(b U a)`**:  
     - *Interpretation*: "Condition `b` must hold continuously **until** condition `a` becomes true, and `a` **must eventually become true**."  
     - *Example*: If `b` is "safety checks are active" and `a` is "operation starts," then `(b U a)` requires safety checks to remain active until operation starts.  

3. **Conjunction Operator (`&`)**:  
   - The top-level `&` requires **both** `(c U a)` and `(b U a)` to be satisfied **simultaneously on the same execution path**.  
   - Crucially, **both subformulas mandate the same event**: `a` must eventually occur.  
   - Since `a` is shared, the first occurrence of `a` **must satisfy both** `(c U a)` and `(b U a)`:  
     - Before `a` occurs, **both `c` and `b` must hold continuously** because:  
       - `(c U a)` enforces `c` until `a`, and `(b U a)` enforces `b` until `a`.  
     - `a` must occur at some future time.  
   - Additional occurrences of `a` after the first are irrelevant (once the first `a` occurs, both `U` formulas are satisfied).  

#### Step 4: Refined Natural Language Phrasing  
The **most accurate and concise translation** is:  
> **"Conditions `c` and `b` must both hold continuously until `a` eventually becomes true."**  

##### Key Clarifications:  
- **"Continuously"**: At every state from the current moment until the state immediately **before** `a` occurs, **both `c` and `b` must be true** (i.e., no gaps).  
- **"Eventually"**: `a` **must occur at least once** in the future. On the state where `a` is true, `c`/`b` **may or may not hold** (they are not required beyond the prior states).  
Omit vague terms like "moment" or "parallel progress" to avoid ambiguity.  

##### Why Simpler Forms Work:  
The formula `(c U a) & (b U a)` is **logically equivalent** to `(c ∧ b) U a`:  
- `(c ∧ b) U a`: "`c` ∧ `b` holds until `a`, with `a` eventually occurring."  
- Both express **identical constraints** over timelines. For brevity, the simplified phrasing captures all critical requirements:  
  - Continuous `c` and `b` before `a`.  
  - Guaranteed occurrence of `a`.  

##### When Is the Formula Satisfied?  
- **True scenario**:  
  - States: `[e]`, `[c, b]`, `[c, b]`, `[a]`.  
  - Explanation: `c` and `b` hold in all states until `a` occurs.  
- **False scenario**:  
  - States: `[c]`, `[c]`, `[a]`.  
  - Explanation: `b` is missing in states before `a`.  

--- 
**Final Translation**  
`(c U a) & (b U a)` means:  
> **"Conditions `c` and `b` must both hold continuously until `a` eventually becomes true."**
