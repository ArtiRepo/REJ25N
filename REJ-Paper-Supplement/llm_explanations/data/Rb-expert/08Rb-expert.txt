### Translation of the LTL Formula: `b U !d`

#### 1. **Original LTL Formula**  
`b U !d`

---

#### 2. **Parsing Tree Breakdown**  
The formula `b U !d` combines two propositions (`b` and `d`) with logical operators:  
- `U`: Ternary temporal operator ("**Until**").  
- `!`: Unary negation operator.  
- `b`/`d`: Atomic propositions (e.g., `b = "button is pressed"`, `d = "door is open"`).  

**Tree Structure**:  
```
      U  
     / \  
    b   !  
        |  
        d  
```  
- **Leaves**: Propositions `b` and `d`.  
- **Negation (`!d`)**: Truth value of `d` is inverted.  
- **Until (`b U !d`)**: Requires `b` to hold *until* `!d` becomes true.  

---

#### 3. **Rebuilding Meaning from the Bottom Up**  

- **Step 1: Atomic propositions**  
  - `b` must hold at the current state.  
  - `d` must hold at the current state (since `!d` requires `d` to be *false*).  

- **Step 2: Negation (`!d`)**  
  - `!d` means "`d` **does not hold**" at a future state.  

- **Step 3: Until (`b U !d`)**  
  - There exists a **future state** (at time `t`) where `!d` is true.  
  - From **now until `t-1`** (all states strictly before `t`), `b` **must hold**.  
  - At time `t`, `b` **may or may not hold**, but `d` **must be false**.  

**Semantics**:  
- If `d` is *already false* at the current state, the formula is **immediately satisfied** (no constraints on `b`).  
- If `d` is true, `b` must hold **continually** until a state where `d` becomes false.  

---

#### 4. **Refined Natural Language Phrasing**  

> "**The condition `d` must eventually become false.**  
> From now **until the moment when `d` is false**, the condition `b` must **hold continuously** at every intermediate state."  

**Key Clarifications**:  
- **"Eventually"**: `d` **must become false at least once** in the future.  
- **"Until the moment when `d` is false"**:  
  - `b` **must hold** at all states leading up to (but *not including*) the state where `d` becomes false.  
  - At the state where `d` becomes false:  
  ```markdown
    - `d` must be false.  
    - `b` may be true or false.  
  ```  
- **Effect of starting state**:  
  - If `d` is false initially, the formula holds **regardless of `b`**.  
  - If `d` is true initially, `b` **must be true now** and **persist** until `d` fails.  

**Example** (for intuition):  
- `b`: "Engine runs," `d`: "Overheating detected."  
- Formula: `engine_runs U !overheating_detected`  
- Translation:  
  *"The engine must run continuously until overheating is **first detected as resolved** (i.e., "overheating_detected becomes false"). Overheating must eventually be resolved."*  

---

### Final Translation  
`b U !d` =  
> **"Eventually `d` must become false, and until that moment, `b` must hold continuously."**
