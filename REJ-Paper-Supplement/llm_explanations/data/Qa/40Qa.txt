  $ F(o \lor u) $

- **Rebuilding Meaning from the Bottom Up**:  
  1. **Atomic propositions**: $ o $ and $ u $ represent specific conditions or events that may hold in a system state.  
  2. **Disjunction $ o \lor u $**: This means "either $ o $ is true, or $ u $ is true, or both" at a particular point in time.  
  3. **Finally operator $ F $**: This imposes that the disjunction $ o \lor u $ must hold at **some point in the future** (i.e., at least once during the system's execution).  

  Combining these, the formula $ F(o \lor u) $ asserts that **at least one of $ o $ or $ u $** must become true at **some future time** during the system's run. It does not require $ o $ or $ u $ to be true forever, only that their disjunction is eventually satisfied.
