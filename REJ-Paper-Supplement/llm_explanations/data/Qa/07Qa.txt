- **Original LTL formula**:  
  `a U c`  

- **Breakdown (parsing tree)**:  
  - Root: `U` (Until operator)  
    - Left child: `a` (proposition)  
    - Right child: `c` (proposition)  

- **Rebuilding meaning from the bottom up**:  
  The formula `a U c` requires that:  
  1. The proposition `a` is true in all states starting from the current time step, **until** the proposition `c` becomes true.  
  2. The proposition `c` **must eventually** become true at some point in the future.  
  In other words, `a` must hold continuously until `c` is satisfied, and `c` cannot be indefinitely false.  

- **Refined natural language phrasing**:  
  `"a must hold continuously until c occurs, and c is guaranteed to happen eventually."`  
  or  
  `"c must eventually become true, and a remains true at every moment before that."`
