- **Original LTL formula**: F (o U u)  
- **Breakdown into a parsing tree**:  
  - **Top operator**: F (Eventually)  
    - **Operand**: (o U u) (o Until u)  
      - **Left operand of U**: o  
      - **Right operand of U**: u  

- **Rebuild meaning from the bottom of the tree up**:  
  1. **"o U u"** means "o holds at all times until u becomes true." For this to be satisfied, there must be some future time where u is true, and o is true for all times before that.  
  2. **"F (o U u)"** adds the "Eventually" operator, meaning that the "o U u" condition must be true at **some specific time in the future**. This implies the system must reach a point where:  
     - At that time, o is true.  
     - From that time onward, o continues to hold until u is true (i.e., u must eventually occur, and o must not fail before u does).  
  3. Together, **F (o U u)** requires the system to have at least one moment where "o persists until u is true," and u must eventually happen.
