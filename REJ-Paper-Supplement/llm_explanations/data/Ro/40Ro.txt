     - `u` means "`u` holds" (or "`u` is true").  
   - **Step 2: Disjunction (OR)**  
     - `(o | u)` means "`o` holds **or** `u` holds" (at the same moment).

     - `F` means "at **some future moment** in the system execution".  
     - Combining with `(o | u)`: "At some future moment, `o` holds **or** `u` holds".

   - **Final translation**:

- **`F` (eventually)**: Focuses on a **future state** (no guarantee when).  
- **`|` (OR)**: Requires **at least one** of `o` or `u` to be true **at that same future moment**.  
- **Translation structure**: Temporal operator (`F`) defines **when**, logical operator (`|`) defines **what must be true** at that time.
