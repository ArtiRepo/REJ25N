### Translation of LTL Formula: **(a U b) & (c U d)**

#### 1. **Original LTL Formula**:  
`(a U b) & (c U d)`

#### 2. **Parsing Tree Breakdown**:  
- Top-level operator: **`&`** (logical AND).  
  - Left operand: **`a U b`** (`U` = Until).  
    - Sub-operands: `a` (left), `b` (right).  
  - Right operand: **`c U d`** (`U` = Until).  
    - Sub-operands: `c` (left), `d` (right).  

```
        &
       /   \
      U     U
     / \   / \
    a   b c   d
```

---

#### 3. **Rebuild Meaning from Bottom Up**:  
1. **Leaf nodes (atomic propositions)**:  
   - `a`, `b`, `c`, `d` are atomic properties (e.g., "event `a` occurs").  

2. **Meaning of `a U b`**:  
   - **Translation**: *"At some future time, `b` must become true, and until then, `a` must hold at every step."*  
   - Semantics:  
     - **Eventuality**: `b` **must eventually hold**.  
     - **Invariance**: From the current time until `b` holds, `a` must **continuously hold** at all intermediate steps.  

3. **Meaning of `c U d`**:  
   - Similarly: *"At some future time, `d` must become true, and until then, `c` must hold at every step."*  

4. **Top-level `&` (AND)**:  
   - Combines both `U`-subformulas:  
     *"The system must satisfy `(a U b)` **and** `(c U d)`."*  

---

#### 4. **Refined Natural Language Phrasing**:  
> **Final Translation**:  
> *"Eventually, `b` must hold and `a` must hold continuously until that time. Simultaneously, eventually,<br> `d` must hold and `c` must hold continuously until that time."*

##### Key Clarifications:  
- **Independence**: The deadlines for `b` and `d` **may occur at different times** (no ordering enforced).  
  *(Example: `b` might occur before `d`, `d` before `b`, or both at the same step).*  
- **Continuity**: `a` must hold **at every step** until `b`â€™s first occurrence (analogous for `c` until `d`).  
- **Eventuality Guarantee**: Both `b` and `d` **must eventually occur** (e.g., the system cannot stall indefinitely).  
- **Concurrency**: Conditions run in parallel on the **same execution path**.
