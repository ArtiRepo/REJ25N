- **Atomic propositions**: `g` and `j` represent system states (e.g., "gate is open", "job is running").  
- **Negations**:  
  - `!g` means "`g` is **false**".  
  - `!j` means "`j` is **false**".  
- **Disjunction (`|`)**: `!g | !j` means "**either `g` is false OR `j` is false**" (or both).  
- **Next (`X`)**: `X(...)` applies to the entire disjunction, meaning "in the **immediately next state**".

#### Rebuilding Semantics (Bottom-Up):
1. **Leaf nodes (`g`, `j`)**:  
   - `g` holds or fails at a given moment.  
   - `j` holds or fails at a given moment.  

2. **Negations (`!g`, `!j`)**:  
   - `!g` constrains the state where `g` is **absent**.  
   - `!j` constrains the state where `j` is **absent**.  

3. **Disjunction (`!g | !j`)**:  
   - The disjunction is satisfied if **at least one** of `!g` or `!j` holds.  
   - This is equivalent to: **"`g` and `j` cannot both be true at the same time"** (by De Morganâ€™s law).  

4. **Next (`X(!g | !j)`)**:  
   - The constraint (`!g | !j`) must hold **specifically in the next immediate state**.  
   - The formula ignores **all other states** (past, present, or future beyond the next step).

#### Natural Language Translation (Refined):
> "**In the immediate next moment, it is not the case that both `g` and `j` are true.**"  

#### Explanation:
- **When is it satisfied?**  
  In any system trace where, at the **very next time step** after evaluation, at least one of these holds:  
  - `g` is false,  
  - `j` is false,  
  - or both are false.  
  Example: If the current time is `t`, the formula requires that `g` or `j` fails at `t+1`.  

- **What constraint does it impose?**  
  - **No restriction** on the **current state** (only the next state matters).  
  - **No guarantee** about any state **beyond the next one** (only `t+1` is constrained).  
  - Forbids the scenario where **`g` and `j` are simultaneously true at `t+1`**.  

#### Alternative Phrasing:
- "**At the next moment, `g` and `j` cannot both hold.**"  
- "**Immediately after now, either `g` fails, `j` fails, or both fail.**"
