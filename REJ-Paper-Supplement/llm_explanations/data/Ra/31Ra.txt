     - **Right child**: `X ( ... )` (the "Next" operator), with one child:
       - `(c U a)` (the "Until" operator), with two children:

3. **Bottom-Up Rebuilding of Meaning**:  
   - **Step 1: Atomic propositions (`a`, `c`, `d`)**  
     - `a`, `c`, `d` represent specific events/states (e.g., `a` = "event A occurs").  
   - **Step 2: "Until" subformula `(c U a)`**  
     - `(c U a)` holds at a time `t` if:  
       (i) `a` eventually becomes true at some future time `k â‰¥ t`, and  
       (ii) `c` is **continuously true** from `t` up to **the time step immediately before `k`**.  
     - **Example**: If `(c U a)` holds now (`t=0`), then `c` must be true at `t=0, 1, 2, ...` until `a` becomes true at `k` (say, `k=3`). At `k=3`, `c` may be true or false.  
   - **Step 3: "Next" operator `X (c U a)`**  
     - `X (c U a)` holds **now** if `(c U a)` holds at the **next time step** (`t=1`).  
     - This means:  
       (i) `a` eventually becomes true at **or after** `t=1`, and  
       (ii) `c` is continuously true **from `t=1`** until the step before `a` occurs.  
   - **Step 4: Conjunction `d & ...`**  
     - The full formula holds now if **both** are true at the **current time** (`t=0`):  
       (i) `d` is true **now** (`t=0`),  
       (ii) `X (c U a)` is true **now** (ensuring `(c U a)` holds from `t=1`).

---
4. **Refined Natural Language Phrasing**:  
   The formula **`d & X (c U a)`** means:  
   > **"`d` is true now, and starting from the very next moment, `c` remains continuously true until the first occurrence of `a`, which must happen eventually at or after the next moment."**  

   - **Key constraints**:  
     - **`d`** must hold **in the current state** (e.g., "`d` is active now").  
     - **`a` must eventually occur**, but **only at or after the next time step** (it cannot happen in the current state).  
     - **`c` must hold continuously** from the next state (`t=1`) until **the moment right before `a` occurs**. At the exact time `a` becomes true, `c` may or may not hold.  
     - "**Continuously true until...**" allows for two cases:  
       1. `a` becomes true at `t=1`: No requirement for `c` (since no time steps precede `t=1`).  
       2. `a` becomes after `t=1` (e.g., at `t=k`): `c` must be true at all times from `t=1` to `t=k-1`.  

   - **Why this matters for the system**:  
     - Forces an immediate constraint: `d` **must be satisfied** now.  
     - Ensures a **future obligation**: From `t=1` onward, `c` persists as a precondition while "waiting" for `a`. If `a` never occurs, the formula is violated.  

---
### Final Translation Summary:
> **"At the current moment, `d` must hold. Then, starting from the next moment, `c` must remain true continuously until `a` first occurs, and `a` must eventually occur at or after that next moment."**
