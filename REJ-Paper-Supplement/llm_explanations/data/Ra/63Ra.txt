`XXXa`

```
XXXa  
├── X   (Outermost operator)  
│   └── XXa  
│       ├── X   (Middle operator)  
│       │   └── Xa  
│       │       ├── X   (Innermost operator)  
│       │       └── a   (Atomic proposition)  
```

1. **Innermost layer `Xa`**:  
   - **Meaning**: "`a` holds at the **next immediate time step**."

   - **Formal**: At `t`, `XXa` requires `Xa` to hold at `t+1`, which means `a` must hold at `(t+1)+1 = t+2`.  
   - **Result**: "`a` holds **two time steps** from now."

3. **Outermost layer `X(XXa)` ≡ `XXXa`**:  
   - **Meaning**: "At the next immediate time step, `XXa` must hold."  
   - **Formal**: At `t`, `XXXa` requires `XXa` to hold at `t+1`, which means `a` must hold at `(t+1)+2 = t+3`.  
   - **Result**: "`a` holds **three time steps** from now."

#### Refined Natural Language Phrasing:
- **Explanation**:  
  The formula `XXXa` is **satisfied** at the current moment if and only if the proposition `a` is **guaranteed to be true exactly three time steps into the future**. It imposes no constraints on the current state, the next state, or the state after that. Only the state **three transitions away** must satisfy `a`.  

- **Key implications**:  
  - If `XXXa` must hold **globally** (e.g., as a system requirement), then at **every point in time**, `a` must hold three steps later. This creates on ongoing chain of constraints:  
    - At time `t=0`, `a` must hold at `t=3`.  
    - At `t=1`, `a` must hold at `t=4`.  
    - At `t=2`, `a` must hold at `t=5`, and so on.  

- **Violation example**:  
  If `a` is false at *any* time `t+3`, then `XXXa` is false at time `t`.

### Final Translation:
> "At the current state, the proposition `a` must be true **exactly three time steps later**."  

This captures the precise temporal constraint while clearly expressing the formula’s meaning and implications.
