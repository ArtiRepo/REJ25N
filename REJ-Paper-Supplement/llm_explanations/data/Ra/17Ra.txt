### 3. **Rebuilding Meaning (Semantics)**
Start from the bottom of the parsing tree upwards:

- **Base `c`**:  
  "Condition `c` holds in the current state."  
  *(Example: "The system is idle.")*

- **Apply `G`**:  
  `G c` binds to `c`, requiring:  
  "Condition `c` holds **in every future state**."  
  *(Example: "The system is idle at all times.")*

- **Apply `!`**:  
  `!G c` negates `G c`, meaning:  
  "It is **not true** that `c` holds in every future state."  
  This implies:  
  "**There exists at least one state** in the future where `c` is **false**."  
  *(Example: "The system is not always idle.")*

  - After some time (if `c` is temporarily true but eventually becomes false).
