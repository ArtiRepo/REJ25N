### Translation and Explanation of the Formula: **G !a**

#### **Natural Language Translation:**  
"The proposition `a` must be **false in every state** of the execution, starting immediately and continuing indefinitely into the future."

---

#### **Key Explanations:**

1. **When Constraints Apply:**  
   - **Applies immediately and continuously**: The formula imposes a constraint on `a` **in every state**, starting from the moment the formula is evaluated.  
   - **Never ceases**: The constraint persists forever; there is no state where `a` is allowed to be true.  
   - **Variables constrained**: Only `a` is constrained. Other variables (e.g., `b`, `c`) are unaffected.  

2. **Evaluation Over Time:**  
   - **Immediate satisfaction**: At the current state, `a` must be false.  
   - **Ongoing constraints**: In every future state (without exception), `a` must remain false.  
   - **Truth-value evolution**:  
     - If `a` is false in all states, the formula holds **forever**.  
     - If `a` becomes true in **any state** (even once), the formula is **violated permanently** from that point onward.  

3. **Satisfying vs. Violating Sequences:**  
   - **Satisfying sequence**: `a` is **always false**.  
     - Example: `[a=false, a=false, a=false, ...]`  
   - **Violating sequence**: `a` is **true in at least one state**.  
     - Example: `[a=false, a=false, a=true, ...]` (violation at the third state).  
     - *Note*: The formula is violated **as soon as `a` becomes true**, and remains violated regardless of subsequent states.  

4. **Potential Misunderstandings:**  
   - **Myth**: "`a` can be true after a long time."  
     - **Clarification**: The constraint **never expires**. `a` must be false **eternally**.  
   - **Myth**: "The formula only cares about the current state."  
     - **Clarification**: It constrains **all states**, including the present and every future state.  
   - **Myth**: "If `a` is false for 99% of states, the formula is mostly satisfied."  
     - **Clarification**: A single `true` value for `a` violates the entire formula irreparably.  

---

#### **Summary of Constraints:**  
| Time Period      | Constraint on `a`       | Constraint on Other Variables |  
|------------------|-------------------------|-------------------------------|  
| **Current state** | `a` must be false.      | None.                        |  
| **All future states** | `a` must be false.      | None.                        |  
| **After violation** | Formula is **permanently false**. | Unconstrained.              |  

---

#### **Final Translation:**  
"`a` must **never** be true, now or at any time in the future."  

This formula expresses a **safety property**, forbidding `a` from ever becoming true. It is **violated instantly** if `a` is true in the current state or **permanently violated** if `a` becomes true later. Once violated, it cannot be satisfied again.
