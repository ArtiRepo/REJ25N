### Translation of LTL Formula: **G t ∨ F w**

#### Step 1: Original Formula  
`G t ∨ F w`  
(where `∨` is logical OR, `G` is "Globally", `F` is "Eventually/Future", `t` and `w` are atomic propositions).

---

#### Step 2: Parsing Tree Breakdown  
```
      ∨
     /   \
    G      F
    |      |
    t      w
```
- **Atomic propositions**:  
  - `t`: Represents a condition that may be true/false at any moment.  
  - `w`: Similar to `t`, an independent condition.  
- **Temporal operators**:  
  - `G t` (Globally `t`): Constrains all future states from the evaluation point onward.  
  - `F w` (Future `w`): Constrains at least one future state.  

---

#### Step 3: Rebuilding Meaning from Bottom Up  
1. **Atomic propositions (`t`, `w`)**  
   - Evaluated at **each individual time step**. No constraints beyond their immediate truth value.  
2. **Subformula `G t` (Globally `t`)**  
   - **Meaning**: `t` must hold **at every moment** from now (`t₀`, `t₁`, `t₂`, ...).  
   - **Constraints**:  
     - Active **for all future states** until the end of time.  
     - Fails if `t` is false **at any future point**, regardless of prior states.  
   - **Termination of constraints**: Never terminates; always requires `t` to be true.  
3. **Subformula `F w` (Future `w`)**  
   - **Meaning**: `w` must be true **at least once** (now or in the future).  
   - **Constraints**:  
     - Active **until `w` first becomes true**.  
     - Ceases immediately after `w` occurs (even if `w` becomes false later).  
   - **Termination of constraints**: When `w` occurs.  
4. **Disjunction `G t ∨ F w`**  
   - **Meaning**: The system must satisfy **at least one** of:  
     - `t` holds **forever**, **OR**  
     - `w` occurs **at least once**.  
   - **Constraints**:  
     - While neither condition holds, the formula remains unfulfilled.  
     - Constraints lift as soon as **one subformula is satisfied**.  

---

#### Step 4: Natural Language Translation  
**Final Translation**:  
>*"Either `t` must hold forever, or `w` must occur at least once."*  

**Refined Explanation**:  
- The formula requires:  
  - **Option 1**: `t` is **always true** (from the current point onward), **or**  
  - **Option 2**: `w` becomes true **sometime** (immediately or later).  
- **Unlike implications**, there is **no direct link** between `t` failing and `w` occurring. The system satisfies the formula if **either condition is met**, regardless of how they interact.  

---

### Constraints and Evolution Over Time  
#### Key Rules:  
| Subformula | Constraint Applies... | Constraints Cease When... | Impact of Mid-Changes |
|------------|------------------------|----------------------------|------------------------|
| **`G t`**  | Immediately and **continuously** for **all future states**. | **Never**—if relied upon, `t` must hold forever. | If `t` becomes false **anywhere**, `G t` is irreversibly violated. |
| **`F w`**  | Immediately but **temporarily** until `w` occurs. | **As soon as `w` is true** (even once). | Once `w` occurs, `F w` is **permanently satisfied** (regardless of later `w` values). |
| **`G t ∨ F w`** | Actively requires one of the two conditions to hold. | Lifts when either `G t` or `F w` is satisfied. | Until satisfied, the system **must preserve the possibility** to fulfill one option. |

#### Satisfying Sequences:  
1. **`t` holds forever**:  
   - `w` may be `true`/`false` arbitrarily (e.g., `t, t, t, ...` or `t, w, !t, ...`).  
2. **`w` occurs at least once**:  
   - `t` may hold forever (e.g., `t, t, w, t, ...`) **or fail** (e.g., `!t, w, ...`, `t, !t, w, ...`).  
   *✓ Formula holds if either scenario happens.*

#### Violating Sequences:  
- **Only when**:  
  - `t` **fails at least once** (violating `G t`), **AND**  
  - `w` **never occurs** (violating `F w`).  
- **Example trace**:  
  `t, t, !t, t, !t, ...` (with `w` always false).  
  `!t` appears, so `G t` fails; `w` never occurs, so `F w` fails → overall violation.  

#### Common Misunderstandings:  
1. **"`G t` implies `w` can be ignored"**:  
   - **False**. If `w` occurs while `t` holds forever, the formula is **still satisfied** (but `G t` alone suffices).  
2. **"`t` must hold until `w` occurs"**:  
   - **False**. The formula does **not require `t` to persist until `w`** (unlike `t U w`). If `w` occurs immediately, `t` can fail freely in the future.  
3. **"Failure of `t` forces `w` to appear"**:  
   - **False**. If `w` **already occurred** before `t` failed, the formula still holds. `F w` needs only **one occurrence** (anytime).  

---

### Summary  
- **Initial evaluation**: True if the entire future trace satisfies `G t` or `F w`.  
- **System requirements**:  
  - If `w` never occurs, `t` must hold **eternally**.  
  - If `t` ever fails, `w` must occur **sometime** (before or after the failure).  
- **Constraint liftoff**: The formula **stops constraining** once either `t` passes all checks (perpetually) or `w` appears once.  

**All cases covered**:  
- **Satisfaction**: `t` holds forever and/or `w` occurs.  
- **Violation**: `t` eventually fails while `w` never occurs.
