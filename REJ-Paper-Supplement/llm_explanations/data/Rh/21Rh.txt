**Original LTL formula:**  
X (t & y)  

**Rebuilding Meaning (bottom-up):**  
1. **Innermost expression `t & y`**:  
   Both propositions `t` and `y` must be true **in the same state**.  

2. **Next operator `X` applied to `(t & y)`**:  
   The conjunction `t & y` must be true **in the state immediately following the current one**.  

**Refined Natural Language Phrasing:**  
- **"In the very next state, both `t` and `y` must be true."**  
- **Constraints**:  
  - **Current state**: No constraints on `t` or `y` (they can be true/false/undefined).  
  - **Next state**: Both `t` and `y` must be true.  
  - **Beyond the next state**: No constraints (the formula stops imposing requirements after the next state).  
- **Satisfaction**: Holds if the next state has `t` and `y` both true.  
- **Violation**: Occurs if `t` or `y` is false in the next state (failure at immediate successor).
