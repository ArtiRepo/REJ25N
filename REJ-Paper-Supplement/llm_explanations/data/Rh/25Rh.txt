### Translation and Explanation for LTL Formula: **`!X t`**

#### Step 1: Original LTL Formula  
`!X t`

#### Step 2: Rebuild Meaning (Bottom-Up)  
1. **Innermost operator `X t`**:  
   - `X t` means "`t` must be true in the **immediately next state**."  

2. **Apply negation (`!`)** to `X t`:  
   - `!X t` flips the requirement: "It is **not** the case that `t` is true in the next state."  
   → Simplified: "**`t` must be false in the next state.**"  

#### Step 3: Refined Natural Language Phrasing  
> **"`t` must be false in the immediately next state."**  

---

### Key Clarifications  
1. **Constraints imposed by `!X t`**:  
   - **Applies exclusively to the _next state_**:  
     - No constraint on `t` in the **current state** (it can be `true` or `false` here).  
     - No constraint on `t` **beyond the next state** (e.g., irrelevant for future states like state 2, 3, ...).  
   - **Violation condition**:  
     The formula is violated **at the current state** if `t` is `true` in the next state.  
   - **Satisfaction condition**:  
     The formula is satisfied **at the current state** if `t` is `false` in the next state.  

2. **Temporal scope**:  
   - The constraint **locks** to the moment the formula is evaluated (e.g., at state \(s_i\)).  
   - It **ceases to apply** after the next state (state \(s_{i+1}\)) is evaluated.  
   - Example sequence:  
     - States: \(s_0\) → \(s_1\) → \(s_2\) → ...  
     - If evaluated at \(s_0\):  
       - **Constraint**: \(t\) **must be false in \(s_1\)**.  
       - \(s_0\) and \(s_2, s_3, ...\) are unconstrained.  
   - If evaluated at \(s_1\):  
     - Constraint shifts to requiring `t` false in \(s_2\) (etc.).  

3. **Common Pitfalls**:  
   - **Not a global constraint**: `!X t` does **not** mean "`t` is always false" (`G¬t`).  
   - **Not about the past or distant future**: Concerned **only** with the direct successor state.  
   - **Immediate action**: Unlike `F` (eventually) or `G` (always), `X` enforces immediate compliance in **one step**.  

---  
**Final Translation**:  
> "`t` must be false in the very next state."
