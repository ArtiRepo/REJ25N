**Original LTL formula:** F a  

**Rebuilding meaning bottom-up:**  
1. **a**: The proposition `a` must hold (be true) in the current state if evaluated immediately as a standalone formula.  
2. **F a**: The operator `F` (eventually) requires that `a` holds in *at least one future state* (including the current state). The truth of `a` in any single state satisfies the formula.  

**Refined natural language phrasing:**  
"Eventually, `a` must be true at least once, now or in the future."  

### Key Explanations:  
- **When constraints apply:**  
  - If `a` is immediately true, **no further constraints** are imposed by this formula for the entire execution (the formula is permanently satisfied).  
  - If `a` is false now, the system **must eventually satisfy `a`** in some future state. Until that occurs, the constraint remains active and binding.  
- **When constraints cease:** All constraints lift permanently as soon as `a` becomes true in any state.  

**Satisfying executions:**  
- `[a=true, ...]` (satisfied immediately).  
- `[a=false, a=false, a=true, ...]` (satisfied at third state).  
- `[a=false, a=true, a=false, ...]` (satisfied at second state; `a` may change later).  

**Violating executions:**  
- `[a=false, a=false, a=false, ...]` (all states false; formula never satisfied).

- The formula **does not require `a` to persist** after its first occurrenceâ€”one `true` value suffices. Constraints end upon its satisfaction.
