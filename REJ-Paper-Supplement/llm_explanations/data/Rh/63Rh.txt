#### Final Natural Language Translation:  
"**a must be true three states from the current state.**"

#### Key Clarifications:
- **When the constraint applies**:  
  The formula **only constrains the third state** from the evaluation point.  
  - It does *not* constrain the current state, the next state (1 step ahead), or the state after that (2 steps ahead).  
  - a may be true/false freely in these earlier states without affecting satisfaction.  

- **Violation condition**:  
  The formula is violated **if the third state exists and has a = false**, or **if fewer than three states exist** (e.g., the system terminates before state 3).  

- **Behavior over time**:  
  - If evaluated **at state S<sub>0</sub>**: Checks a at S<sub>3</sub>.  
  - If evaluated **at state S<sub>1</sub>**: The same formula would check a at S<sub>4</sub> (context shifts).  
  - **Once S<sub>3</sub> passes**, the formula is resolved (no retrospective constraints).
