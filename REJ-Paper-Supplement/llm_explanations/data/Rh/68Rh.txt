### Translation of LTL Formula: `a U b`

#### Original Formula:  
`a U b`

#### Translation Reasoning:  
1. **Break down the `U` (Until) operator**:  
   - `a U b` requires:  
     - **Eventuality constraint**: `b` *must* become true at least once in the future.  
     - **Safety constraint**: Until the *first occurrence* of `b`, `a` *must* be true in **every state** (including the current state if `b` is not yet true).  

2. **Constraints and their application**:  
   - **If `b` is true now** (current state):  
     - The formula is **immediately satisfied**. No requirement on `a` in the current or future states.  
   - **If `b` is false now**:  
     - `a` *must* be true in the **current state**.  
     - `a` *must* hold **continuously** in every subsequent state until the **very first state** where `b` becomes true.  
     - The first occurrence of `b` **releases** the obligation for `a` (no need for `a` to hold after this point).

     - Case 3: `a` holds until some states, but fails *before* the first `b` (e.g., `a=false` occurs earlier than `b`).  

4. **Key Clarifications**:  
   - The constraint on `a` applies **only until the first occurrence** of `b`.  
   - **After `b` first becomes true**: No further constraints on `a` or `b` (later `a=false` or repeated `b` does not invalidate the formula).  
   - "Eventually" requires `b` to occur at least once; "every state" in the safety phase is strict (no gaps).  

#### Final Natural Language Translation:  
> "`a U b` requires `b` to eventually become true (at least once). From the current state onward, `a` must hold continuously until the **first occurrence** of `b` is reached. Once `b` occurs, `a` is no longer constrained."
