### Original LTL Formula:
**G((a and Xa) → XX¬a)**

### Step-by-Step Translation:

**1. Base Components:**
- **`a`**: The proposition `a` holds in the *current state*.
- **`Xa`**: The proposition `a` holds in the *immediately next state*.
- **`a and Xa`**: **Both `a` holds now and in the next state** (consecutive `a` at current and next index).
- **`XX¬a`**: **Two states after the current, `a` is false**.
- **`(a and Xa) → XX¬a`**: **If `a` holds now and next, then `a` must be false two states later** (logical implication).  
  → If there are two consecutive `a` states starting now, the state immediately after them must break the sequence with `¬a`.  
  → Otherwise (if the condition fails, i.e., `a` is false now *or* next), the formula **imposes no constraint on the state two steps ahead**.

**2. Global Operator `G`**:  
- Apply `(a and Xa) → XX¬a` **to every state** in the execution.

### Final Natural Language Translation:
**"Globally, if two consecutive states satisfy `a`, the state two steps after the first of these must violate `a`. This forbids any three consecutive `a` states."**  

---

### Key Implications:
1. **Constraint Trigger:**  
   - Only active in states where `a` holds **now** *and* **next** (consecutive `a`).  
   - **If triggered**: Forces **`¬a` two states into the future**.  
   - **Else**: No constraints on `a` beyond normal behavior.

2. **Temporal Evolution:**  
   - **Satisfaction**: The formula **holds** if:  
     - No two consecutive `a` occur, *or*  
     - After any pair of consecutive `a`, the very next state is `¬a`.
     - *(Example valid sequence: `a, a, ¬a, ...`)*.  
   - **Violation**: The formula **fails** if three consecutive `a` occur at any point.  
     *(Example invalid sequence: `a, a, a, ...` → fails at the first state of an `a, a` pair as the third state violates `XX¬a`)*.  

3. **Effect**:  
   - Ensures `a` **never holds for three or more consecutive states**.  
   - Short sequences of `a` (length ≤2) are allowed if separated by `¬a`.

---  
**Distilled Summary:**  
*"`a` may occur singly or in pairs, but never in triplets."*
